The Sovereign Stack Framework

A layered approach to reducing asymmetric dependency on US institutional capacity:

  • Layer 1 ‚Äî Data independence: Mirror and cache critical US datasets (weather, geospatial, health); fund Canadian observation networks
  • Layer 2 ‚Äî Standards independence: Where US agencies are being hollowed out, align with EU/ISO/allied standards instead of waiting for US convergence
  • Layer 3 ‚Äî Procurement independence: Buy Canadian + Commonwealth + allied procurement panels; reduce single-source US vendor dependency
  • Layer 4 ‚Äî Financial independence: Reduce tariff exposure through trade diversification + supply-chain redundancy for critical inputs (steel, aluminum, critical minerals)
  • Layer 5 ‚Äî Institutional independence: Build Canadian expertise in domains where US federal capacity is being dismantled; offer to host regional multilateral bodies

CUSMA 2026 Review ‚Äî Policy Actions

Scenario A ‚Äî Extension agreed:

Continue with current framework; use review window to address supply-chain vulnerabilities identified in 2025 tariff shocks.

Scenario B ‚Äî US withdraws threat / instability:

All contracts, procurement programs, and investment plans should already have alternative compliance pathways. Model the 6-month notice period now.

Scenario C ‚Äî Renegotiated with new terms:

Digital services, AI governance, and supply-chain rules will be contested. Build negotiating leverage through completed diversification moves.

‚ñ∂Canadian CPO Quick-Start Checklist (2026 Edition)copy/paste
  • Procurement: Require audit logs, incident response, and evaluation artifacts before deployment (especially AI/cyber). Apply Buy Canadian criteria.
  • Trade exposure: Map your top 10 US-sourced inputs; identify alternatives in allied markets; model the 35% tariff scenario on your supply chain.
  • Data dependencies: List every external data feed you rely on; flag US federal agency sources; build alternatives or caches.
  • Delivery: Timebox permitting steps; publish "why delayed?" dashboards; use CUSMA-neutral delivery partners where possible.
  • Workforce: Standardize roles; fund pipelines; measure readiness monthly; don't assume US talent mobility continues at historic rates.
  • Continuity: Ring-fence essential services in multi-year budgets; run annual tabletop exercises modelling US partner unavailability.
  • Trust: Publish uncertainty ranges; prebunk; show "what would change our mind?" Distinguish Canadian institutions from US volatility explicitly in public comms.
